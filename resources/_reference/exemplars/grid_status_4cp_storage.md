## Form Lessons (what to imitate)

1. **Dek does real work**: two sentences — what the piece explores + why the reader should care (risk). Our "informative subtitle" spec is exactly this.
2. **Section heads carry personality, body stays analytical** ("Tx Gon' Give It To Ya" · "Show Me the Money") — playfulness is *rationed to headers*; the prose under them is precise. Adopt at lower dose: our voice (`_principles/voice.md`) is more sober, but heads may have life.
3. **The money section**: one section that converts the mechanism into dollars *for a dated, specific case* — quantification is scoped to an observed event, never a forward guess. That is our altitude rule in the wild.
4. **A chart at every statistic** (~10), inline, captions woven into narrative.
5. **The regulatory watch-item close**: ends on "the legislature stirs" + "outcome far from certain" — a forward hook with explicit uncertainty, then a soft product pointer. Maps to our move 8 (watch) + footer.
6. **Hedge vocabulary**: "likely," "may have," "far from certain," "(currently in flux)" — caveats inline, not corralled into a disclaimer paragraph (we keep our amber box AND inline hedges).
