## TL;DR

Red Dog is a **single-point-of-failure energy-and-logistics asset** in the fastest-warming region on Earth. Everything the mine needs (a year of diesel) and everything it sells (its entire zinc/lead concentrate) moves through **one gravel road to one shallow Chukchi-Sea port that is ice-free only ~100 days a year** — so the same shrinking-and-stormier open-water window gates **both** energy-in and revenue-out. That coupling is the climate exposure the existing risk deck never framed, and it is squarely InfraSure's lane. Layered on top is a **permafrost-driven water-balance crisis** that a US regulator has formally conceded *cannot be fixed* (a 10-year compliance runway to ~2036), pushing a **~$279M adaptation capex onto a mine with only ~6 years of ore left**, an escalating royalty, and an owner mid-merger into a copper-focused major. The decision-relevant tail risk — a diesel-tank or tailings-dam failure on thawing ground — is exactly the geometry that **destroyed Norilsk in 2020 (~$2B)**, and the asset-level numbers that would price it (fuel autonomy, dam factor-of-safety, foundation engineering) are **not disclosed** — a finding in itself.

## 1 · The asset in one screen

```text
Red Dog Mine — world's largest zinc mine · Teck Resources operates · NANA Regional Corp. (Iñupiat) owns the land
  NW Arctic Alaska, ~68.1°N (170 km N of the Arctic Circle) · open-pit zinc/lead · runs 24/7/365 · air-only access
  OFF-GRID: ~25 MW typical demand on diesel gensets (installed capacity contested 24.5–40+ MW)
  ~52-mi state-owned (AIDEA) gravel haul road  →  one shallow Chukchi port (no dock; barges lighter to ships offshore)
  FY2025: 462,700 t contained zinc (↓~17% from 555,600 t in 2024, on falling grades) · ore life ~2032
```
*Sources: Alaska DNR; NS Energy; AIDEA; FindAPort; Teck FY2025 production (Feb 2026). Confidence: high (the contested item — generator nameplate — is flagged in §6).*

## 2 · The lead exposure — a coupled energy-and-logistics chokepoint

This is the framing the LT-Risk deck missed, and it's the most decision-relevant climate linkage at the asset:

```text
ONE ~100-day ice-free window (≈ July–October) carries BOTH:
   ENERGY-IN     the year's diesel — ~15.5M gal/yr (mine) + ~1.4M (transport); ~40,000 gal/day; barged into 6 port tanks
   REVENUE-OUT   the entire annual concentrate export — mine produces year-round, stockpiles, ships only in the window
→ a short, late, or stormy open-water season threatens fuel security AND revenue timing simultaneously.
```

**This is not hypothetical.** In late 2025, *"weather and sea conditions"* delayed shipments into early 2026, and **Q4-2025 Red Dog gross profit fell to ~$200M (from ~$303M a year earlier)** on the timing — climate variability already moving the P&L through the logistics window. *(Teck Q4-2025 results, Feb 2026 — fact/high.)*

Two structural amplifiers:
- **Sea-ice retreat cuts both ways**: it can lengthen the shipping window (operational upside) *but* removes the landfast-ice buffer that protected the coast — the same coastal cell is eroding fast at nearby Kivalina. The port relies on **offshore lightering**, so it is exposed to *sea state and storms*, not just ice. *(AIDEA; Ground Truth Alaska — fact/medium.)*
- **The single-window dependency is undisclosed at the point that matters**: how many days of fuel the site can run if a resupply season is missed or cut short is **not public** (see §6). An 8.8 MW solar array (Tugliq, ~2 MW summer-effective, tax-credit-timed to start mid-2026) dents diesel but does not change the resupply dependency. *(Alaska Beacon, Mar 2026 — fact/high; the "dents not displaces" read is ours.)*

## 3 · Permafrost → a water-balance crisis the regulator calls unfixable

The second pillar is documented in **primary regulatory text**, not just press:

```text
permafrost thaw → naturally elevated TDS in receiving waters → lost mixing-zone capacity → discharge blocked
   +  a wetter Arctic → tailings/contact water +60% (3.3B → 5.3B gallons, 2017–2021) → encroaches on dam freeboard
```

- Alaska DEC is **reissuing the discharge permit (APDES AK0038652) with a 10-year compliance-schedule extension** (public-noticed; effective after May 19, 2026, to ~2036), stating in its own Statement of Basis (2026-04-09) that *"there is no reasonably available remedy to the thawing of permafrost and its impact on the receiving waters."* A US regulator formally conceding a Clean-Water-Act limit can't be met because of permafrost is **decision-relevant and unusual**. *(ADEC SOB — fact/high.)* **Note**: this corrects the deck's "permit expiring 2026" — it was *extended*, and the runway now *outlives the ~2032 ore life*, shifting the binding constraint toward closure-phase water treatment.
- ADEC states stored water *"threatens the safety of the tailings impoundment dams,"* with a tail risk of *"dam failure"* / untreated-drainage release. The **Aug-2012 storm (15.2 in rain) exceeded the tailings dam's design flood** — the infrastructure has already operated outside its design envelope. *(ADEC SOB — fact/high; this is the single strongest data point that the asset is past-envelope.)*
- The mitigation under evaluation is a **~$279M, ~50-mile pipeline to the Chukchi Sea** — *"more than half of Red Dog's annual gross profit"* — one of four options, **not committed**. ~$19M was already spent on thaw-driven wastewater in 2020 alone. *(Northern Journal Dec 2025; Alaska Public Media 2020; ADEC SOB — fact/high; the pipeline is an estimate under evaluation — projection.)*
- The regional driver is real and independent of the mine: **"river rusting"** — thaw-exposed sulfide weathering turning 75+ (200+) Brooks Range streams orange — with on-site seeps measured at **pH 3.2 and TDS to 11,300 mg/L**. *(O'Donnell 2024 / NOAA Arctic Report Card 2025; ADEC SOB — fact/high.)*

Context for the *rate*: the Arctic has warmed **~4× the global average** since 1979, and climate models *under*-state it — so the signal arrives faster than scenario planning assumes, compressing adaptation lead-time. *(Rantanen 2022, Nature Comms — fact/high.)*

## 4 · The decision frame — a fading asset asked to fund a costly adaptation

What makes this a genuine decision (not just a hazard list) is the collision of climate capex with late-life economics:

```text
A FADING asset           ore ~2032; Red Dog zinc guided DOWN 2026 375–415 kt → 2027 330–370 → 2028 230–270 kt; extension (Aktigiruq/Anarraaq) only pre-feasibility
A rising cost base       NANA royalty ratchets 40% (2022) → 45% (Oct 2027) → 50% max, as grades/tonnage fall
Ownership in flux        Anglo–Teck merger announced Sept 2025, shareholder-approved Dec 2025; Canada + S. Korea cleared,
                         ONLY CHINA remains (as of Jun 2026), close ~Q3-2026–Q1-2027 → Red Dog a non-core zinc slice of a >70%-COPPER major
A long-tail liability     state closure assurance ~$558M + PERPETUAL post-closure water treatment (outlives the revenue)
```
*Sources: Teck production guidance + MD&A; North of 60 Mining News; Teck/Anglo merger releases; Alaska DNR. Confidence: high (figures), with mine-life ~2032 and the extension as projections.*

The sharp frame, from the Arctic-infrastructure literature: **near-term permafrost risk is largely mitigation-insensitive** — for an *existing* asset, **adaptation, not decarbonization, is the only lever** *(Hjort et al. 2018, Nature Comms)*. So the question Red Dog's owner actually faces is **how much adaptation capex to sink into a depleting, soon-to-change-hands asset** — a ~$279M pipeline (and "hundreds of millions over a decade" for a permanent fix) against ~6 years of declining ore. That tension — not a generic risk rating — is the read a decision-maker needs.

## 5 · Why this generalizes — the outlier class + a realized precedent

Red Dog instantiates a class InfraSure will meet again: the **remote, off-grid, cold-region industrial asset** — *islanded on a diesel microgrid* + *dependent on a single climate-sensitive logistics window* + *built on degrading permafrost*. The method transfers to the next one (another mine, a port, a remote data center).

- **The realized precedent — Norilsk, 2020**: a diesel storage tank on thaw-weakened permafrost piles collapsed → **~21,000 t spill, ~$2B in damages** — the *exact* Red Dog risk geometry, realized. *(ERM / press — fact/high.)* This is why the **fuel-farm foundation engineering** (§6) is the most important number we don't have.
- **The mirror precedent — winter ice roads** (Tibbitt–Contwoyto, serving NWT diamond mines): the same narrowing-seasonal-window risk, *opposite sign* (they need ice *present*; Red Dog needs it *absent*) — showing the "single climate-sensitive window" is the portable risk concept. *(Reuters — fact/high.)*

Frameworks that anchor the class: **IPCC AR6 WG2 CCP6** ("warming and wetting"; most Arctic infrastructure impacted by mid-century, high confidence); **Hjort 2018** (33–69% of pan-Arctic infrastructure on thawing permafrost by 2050); **AMAP/SWIPA**.

## 6 · What we can't know — and why that's a finding

A disciplined read names its blind spots rather than guessing. For Red Dog, the **most decision-relevant numbers are not public**:

```text
· DAYS OF FUEL AUTONOMY if an open-water window is missed/short — undisclosed (the latent single-point-of-failure)
· TAILINGS-DAM factor-of-safety / current freeboard margin — undisclosed (ADEC says it's threatened; the margin isn't public)
· FUEL-FARM FOUNDATION engineering (piles to bedrock? thermosyphons?) — undisclosed (this is exactly the Norilsk failure mode)
· ASSET-LEVEL FINANCIALS — Red Dog has no standalone public P&L (reported inside Teck's zinc segment; ~CAD $846M of the
  segment's $1,143M FY2025 gross-profit-before-D&A is the closest anchor) — so margin-at-risk can't be priced precisely
```
*These gaps are the honest boundary of a research-grounded read — and the precise questions a pilot/data-exchange would close.*
